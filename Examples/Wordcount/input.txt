The World Wide Web, commonly known as the web, is a system of interlinked hypertext documents accessed via the Internet. It enables the retrieval and display of text, images, videos, and other multimedia content by using web browsers. The web was developed in the late 1980s by Tim Berners-Lee while working at CERN in Switzerland. Since then, it has revolutionized the way information is shared and accessed globally.

Today, the web is an indispensable part of daily life, used for communication, entertainment, education, business, and much more. It provides access to vast amounts of information on virtually any topic imaginable. From news and research articles to social media platforms and online shopping, the web offers a wide array of services and resources.

The evolution of the web has led to the emergence of various technologies and standards, including HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and HTTP. These technologies form the foundation of modern web development and enable the creation of dynamic and interactive web pages and applications.

As the web continues to evolve, new trends and innovations emerge, shaping the way we interact with digital content. Mobile devices, responsive design, cloud computing, and artificial intelligence are just a few examples of the advancements driving the future of the web.

In summary, the web has transformed the world by facilitating the exchange of information and ideas on a global scale. It has become an essential tool for individuals, businesses, and organizations worldwide, powering innovation, collaboration, and progress.
